What is Sex or Sexistential Coaching?

Sexistential coaching, also called Sex, Intimacy & Relationship Coaching, focuses on sexuality and supports our human experience. PAUSE! Let me clarify one really important thing: sexuality is deeper than sex. Go ahead and read that again please, take a deep breath, and carry on.

We live in a strangely ironic society: a hypersexualized culture that sends us the message that we should all be having amazing, and endless sex; yet simultaneously treats sex and sexuality as taboo, shameful, or improper to speak about, and cancels the nature of humanity—therefore sexuality: expansiveness. I’m talking about the full continuity of sexuality and sex, from and to passing through everything in between and all the combinations!

In reality, we are sexual beings from birth to death, not because we’re meant to be sexualized at all times, but because we’re wired for connection, pleasure, and embodiment. No one is owed another person, but everyone is entitled to their own desire, their own pleasure, and their own consensual self-expression. These impulses aren’t problems to hide or distrust; they’re parts of being human. When we bring the “forbidden” into the light, especially in a society that’s both obsessed with sex and terrified of talking about it, we liberate us All: we make space for authenticity, “safety”/healing*, and the ability to shine, reducing harm.

*An important note on “safety”:

I use this word with intention. My work is rooted in reclaiming humanity within a society that often tries to strip it away. And acknowledging my humanity means acknowledging my limits: I cannot be a perfectly safe space for everyone, and every being in every moment. The same is true when I recognize the humanity of others: we are all capable of care, and we are all capable of harm.

What I am committed to is this: I do not choose conscious harm, and when harm happens, I am committed to reduce it, be accountable, and repair.

For that reason, I speak less about creating a “safe” space and more about cultivating a healing-oriented space, one shaped by intention, awareness, repair, and humanity rather than the impossible ideal of perfect safety.

What’s the difference between (Sex) Coaching and (Sex) Therapy?

Coaching is distinct from Therapy, though it can complement therapeutic or healing work, and is both educational and transformational. Coaching and Therapy are 100% compatible and complement each other extremely well. However, the two often get confused, so here’s the simple breakdown:

Sex Coaching focuses on skills, education, and forward progress. It is present- and future-focused, and action-oriented—using exercises, communication tools, and guided practices. This modality is about improvement and overcoming roadblocks. Sex Coaching usually requires a certified sex coach or (clinical) sexologist. Sex Coaching is great for individuals or couples wanting better connection, pleasure, intimacy, and/or confidence.

Sex Therapy focuses on mental health or clinical concerns. There could be diagnosing and treatment in therapy. This modality helps with deeper emotional wounds, traumas, and past experiences. Sex Therapy often requires a licensed therapist. Sex Therapy is great for those whose sexual challenges are rooted in emotional, psychological, or trauma-related experiences.

Example of a Sex Coaching question versus Sex Therapy question:
Coaching: “How can I build the sex life I want?”
Therapy: “Why am I struggling, and what deeper healing is needed?”
