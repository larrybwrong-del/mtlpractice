It’s intimacy, relationship, and sex coaching that supports your full human experience. Sexuality is deeper than sex: it's about our innate need for connection, pleasure, belonging, and embodiment. We live in a society obsessed with sex yet terrified of talking about it honestly. This creates shame, inauthenticity, and . In our sessions, we will:

  • Explore your desires, boundaries, and beliefs in a healing-oriented, non-judgmental space.
  • Practice communication and tune into your mind, body, and essence/spirit.

What’s the difference between (Sex) Coaching and (Sex) Therapy?

Coaching is distinct from Therapy, though it can complement therapeutic or healing work, and is both educational and transformational. Coaching and Therapy are 100% compatible and complement each other extremely well. However, the two often get confused, so here’s the simple breakdown:

Sex Coaching focuses on skills, education, and forward progress. It is present- and future-focused, and action-oriented—using exercises, communication tools, and guided practices. This modality is about improvement and overcoming roadblocks. Sex Coaching usually requires a certified sex coach or (clinical) sexologist. Sex Coaching is great for individuals or couples wanting better connection, pleasure, intimacy, and/or confidence.

Sex Therapy focuses on mental health or clinical concerns. There could be diagnosing and treatment in therapy. This modality helps with deeper emotional wounds, traumas, and past experiences. Sex Therapy often requires a licensed therapist. Sex Therapy is great for those whose sexual challenges are rooted in emotional, psychological, or trauma-related experiences.

Example of a Sex Coaching question versus Sex Therapy question:
Coaching: “How can I build the sex life I want?”
Therapy: “Why am I struggling, and what deeper healing is needed?”
